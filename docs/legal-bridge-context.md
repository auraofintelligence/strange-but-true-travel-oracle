# Legal Bridge Context

This note connects the Travel Oracle to the local `legal-memory-workbench` repo.

The point is not to turn the travel site into a lawyer. The point is to prepare cleaner legal-information questions before Luke moves between rule systems.

## Home Rule Layers

Start with Luke's current legal memory:

- Australian / Commonwealth layer
- Queensland layer
- local council, home-base or community layer
- existing personal legal-memory notes, if relevant

These are placeholders for mapping. They are not a claim that every issue has exactly three legal layers.

## Destination Rule Layers

For every country, island, territory or route, map:

- entry, stay, movement and restricted-region rules
- work, business, speaking, volunteering and remote-work rules
- money, tax, banking, customs, crypto and fundraising clues
- relationship, marriage, consent, public affection, family and gender-norm clues
- speech, filming, publishing, sacred-place and reputation risks
- AI, devices, data, drones, biometrics and online conduct
- medicine, health declarations, prescriptions and local substance rules
- police, dispute, complaint, embassy or legal-help pathways

## Source Priority

Prefer official sources:

- embassy or consulate
- immigration or border agency
- police, customs, tax, health or transport regulator
- court, tribunal, ombudsman or public legal-help body
- local government or territory administration

Use community explainers only as clues pointing back to official sources.

## Boundaries

- This is legal information preparation, not legal advice.
- Ask a local lawyer, embassy, consulate or legal clinic when the action is high-risk.
- Insurance only belongs here where it is a legal, visa, entry, venue, transport, event, client or contract requirement.
- Relationship and GGM notes must stay private unless explicitly redacted.
- Do not store passport numbers, active travel bookings, exact live routes or legal case documents in public-facing files.
